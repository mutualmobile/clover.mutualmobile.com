MM Dashboard

The Dashboard

The dashboard screen can show a summary of your sales on a daily, weekly or monthly view. You may tap anywhere along the graph to see a popover of the sales for that point in time. Underneath the timeline graph you will see a summary of total sales, orders per hour, sales per customer, total orders, sales per employee, and sales per hour.

Navigation and Logout

To navigate between the Dashboard, Employee Summary and Product Summary, simply click on the main menu button in the top left-hand corner and select a new page. To log out of the Dashboard application, click the “logout” button from the main menu drop-down seen above.

Expand Date Range

To look at a previous period’s sales simply click on the left and right arrows on the top of the screen to look at past history.

Updating Time Interval

To change the time interval used in Dashboard, simply click on the drop down arrow on the top right-hand side of the screen that lists the interval and select a new time interval. The screen will automatically adjust to show the new interval of data.

Sales Insight

On the lower half of the screen are graphs for “Sales by Category and “Sales by Employee”. When on a desktop or tablet, hovering or tapping over these graphs will show a summary of your top items. Tap “See More” to be taken into an additional details screen with a summary view of each top item and further details about it.

Employees

On the employees screen you can see a snapshot of all of your employees ranked by amount of sales. Click on an individual employee to view more data about what was sold, how many of each item was sold, and what percentage each sale is.

Products

On the products screen you can see an overview of all your products ranked by amount of sales. Click on an individual product to view more data about how much was sold, by whom and what percentage each sale is.

Before You Begin

Make sure your scale is set up and connected to the Clover base station.

Set the SW-RS20 scale to enable serial mode

With the scale turned off press and hold the “ZERO” key and turn the scale on. When “U SEt” appears release the “ZERO” key. Press the “ZERO” key and the display will show “tyP X”, where X is the current type setting. Use the “TARE” key to select 4. Then press “ZERO”, “ZERO” then “ZERO” to exit the setup.

Set prices for weight based items per oz

Currently only ounces is supported; support for more units coming soon. If you choose a measure other than oz the “Unit OZ” at the bottom left corner will be greyed out and all units displayed on order will be automatically converted to oz.

Refrain from adding modifiers on weight based items

Modifiers will be ignored on weight based items in Weigh & Pay.

Modifiers: only a single modifier can be added to an item

Consider setting up modifiers as products that can be added on to an order.

Create Tare Weights (optional)

Creating a tare weight allows you to automatically subtract the weight of containers from your orders.

1. Tap “Edit Container” 2. All your items sold by weight will appear as container options 3. Tap the item you want to add a container for 4. Place the empty container on the scale 5. Tap “Add Container” 6. Give your container a name (e.g. Small) and tap the green check mark 7. Tap done

Using Weigh & Pay

The Main Screen

The main screen is set up with three sections: the scale read-out is on the left edge, your menu of items is in the center, and a ticket for an order is on the right

Scale

The scale read-out updates automatically when items are placed onto or removed from the connected scale.

If the scale is not connected, a red triangle appears at the top of the screen, over the top edge of the scale.

If you don’t have anything on the scale and try to add weight items to your order, a red indicator will highlight the scale reading of 0.0.

If you’re not connected to the network a red triangle will appear at the top of the screen.

Menu

Categories in your menu are listed at the top of the menu section. The first category is ‘All’, which shows all of your menu items.

Switch categories by either tapping the category name or swiping on the screen.

If you have more than three categories, there will be an arrow next to the third category. Tap the arrow to access the list of additional categories.

Swiping on the screen will also navigate through any additional categories.

Items are listed with items sold by weight first, then fixed price items.

Ticket

The ticket lists the items added to the ticket, applicable tax and the total due.

  1. To add an item sold by weight to a ticket:

    1. Place the item on the scale. If the customer is buying multiple containers of the same product sold by weight, they can all be placed on the scale at the same time.
    2. Tap the item in the menu If containers are set up, select the menu item with the correct container size. If containers are used, and multiple containers are on the scale, tap the item with the correct container size the number of times that it is on the scale.
    3. The item will be added to the ticket section of the screen with the weight and price. If containers are used, the tare weight of the container(s) will be subtracted from the scale read-out.

  1. To add fixed price items to a ticket:

    1. Tap the item in the menu.
    2. If there is a modifier, then a list of modifiers will be shown. Tap the appropriate modifier.
    3. The item will be added to the ticket section of the screen with the quantity, price and any modification.
    4. If the same item (with the same modifier, if applicable) is tapped again, the quantity number will increment

Discount and Delete

To add a discount to an entire order:

  1. Tap ‘Order Options’ at the top of the ticket section
  2. In the list that appears, tap ‘Discounts’
  3. A list of discounts you offer will be shown. Tap the appropriate discount.
  4. The discount will be added towards the bottom of the ticket view.

To delete an entire order:

  1. Tap ‘Order Options’ at the top of the ticket section.
  2. In the list that appears, tap ‘Delete’.

To add a discount on an individual item:

  1. Tap the item in the ticket
  2. In the list that appears, tap ‘Discounts’
  3. A list of discounts you offer will be shown. Tap the appropriate discount.
  4. The discount will be added underneath the item

To delete an item from an order:

  1. Tap the item to be removed in the ticket
  2. In the list that appears, tap ‘Delete Item’

Checkout

When a ticket is complete, and the customer is ready to pay, tap the ‘Pay Now’ button at the bottom of the ticket section.

Tapping “Pay Now” takes you to the Clover’s main Register application to accept payment and complete the order. Once payment is complete, tap Done, and you will be switched back to the Weigh & Pay application to start a new order or pull up a recent order.

Save Order

To save an in progress order, and start a new one, tap ‘ New Order’. Retrieve the saved order by going to Recent Orders.

Recent Orders

Recent Orders shows your recent orders. The plus on the right can be used to create a new order. The most recently edited and unpaid order is second from the right. Moving to the left, the orders are unpaid and older followed by paid.

Unpaid orders have a ‘Pay Now’ button at the bottom. Tap the ‘Pay Now’ button to go straight to payment. Tap anywhere else in the order to open the order in the Main Screen to edit the order.

Tapping a paid order will open up the Clover Orders application. You can use Orders to reprint receipts, add tips, process refunds, exchanges and more.
